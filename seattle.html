project:

Information needs for small business owners in Seattle

Database design
Information flow

As the leader of my school's yearbook team, I was in charge of coordinating workflow. This was often difficult across a large team.

The system used for the team was a spreadsheet that listed all pages of the yearbook. After working with this spreadsheet, I identified the following limitations:

  • Team members had to frequently scroll through an extremely long list with hundreds of pages listed which were mostly irrelevant to the specific team member
  • Team members often did not indicate they finished their work and were inconsistent in how they indicated this, slowing down workflow.
  • There was often confusion and delays when a new page was assigned or changed, since many team members do not regularly check the whole spreadsheet
  • Hard to visualize overall progress of what work was complete

To solve these problems, I set out to create a online portal backed by a database of yearbook pages, which would help improve workflow and miscommunication with each team member only seeing what was relevant to them and receiving automatic email notifications when something relevant to them was updated.
